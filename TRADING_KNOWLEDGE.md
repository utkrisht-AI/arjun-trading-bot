## 1. TRADING PSYCHOLOGY & DISCIPLINE

### Source: *Trading in the Zone* — Mark Douglas

**The Five Fundamental Truths of Trading:**
1. Anything can happen in markets — never assume certainty
2. You don't need to predict markets to make money
3. Wins and losses are randomly distributed within an edge
4. An edge is just a higher probability — not a guarantee
5. Every trade is unique — past trades don't define the next

**Seven Principles of Consistency:**
1. Know your statistical edge *before* placing any trade
2. Determine your maximum acceptable loss *before* entry — always
3. Follow rules consistently without attachment to specific outcomes
4. Think in probabilities, not individual trade outcomes
5. Accept that losses are a normal cost of doing business
6. Let disciplined execution become automatic, not effortful
7. Build a probability-based mindset — abandon the need to be "right"

> **Key insight:** Trading success is 80% psychology, 20% strategy.
> Most traders have decent strategies but fail on the mental execution.

---

### Source: *The Disciplined Trader* — Mark Douglas

**The Risk Acceptance Framework:**
- Before entering, fully *and emotionally* accept the potential loss
- Only risk money you're genuinely okay losing
- If a loss would cause significant emotional distress → position is too large
- Goal: not to avoid losses, but to keep them small enough they don't wipe you out

**Revenge Trading & FOMO — The Two Killers:**

| Trap | Trigger | Rule |
|------|---------|------|
| Revenge Trading | Loss → impulse to recover quickly | Take 30-min break after any loss before next trade |
| FOMO | Fear of missing the next big move | Wait for your setup — if you missed it, skip it |
| Overtrading | Too many hours at screen | Set a daily trade limit (e.g., max 3 trades/day) |

**Why emotions hijack decisions:**
- Pain of a loss is psychologically ~2× more powerful than pleasure of equivalent gain (Kahneman's Prospect Theory)
- Financial stress activates the amygdala's fight-or-flight response — making impulsive actions more likely
- **System**: Build rules that *prevent* emotions from hijacking decisions, not rules that require you to suppress them

**Discipline Rules:**
- ⚡ Mandatory cooling-off: 30 minutes after a stopped-out trade before next entry
- ⚡ Auto-reduce position size on next trade after a loss (rebuilds confidence with smaller risk)
- 🧠 Keep a trading journal: date, setup, entry reason, exit reason, emotional state, outcome
- 🧠 Review journal weekly — identify emotional patterns, not just P&L

## 2. ENTRY RULES & SETUPS

### Source: Jesse Livermore — *Reminiscences of a Stock Operator*

**Livermore's Entry Commandments:**
1. ⚡ **Only enter AFTER market action confirms your opinion** — never on opinion alone
2. Trade in the direction of the overall market — never fight the trend
3. Wait for *pivotal points* — specific price levels where a stock either breaks out or fails
4. Volume MUST increase on breakout candles — low-volume breakouts are traps
5. The first few bars after a breakout should move in breakout direction — if they don't, exit
6. If a trade doesn't move in your favor within a few days → exit, no hesitation

> "It never was my thinking that made the big money for me.
>  It was always my sitting. My sitting tight."
> — Jesse Livermore

**CAN SLIM — 7 Stock Selection Criteria:**

| Letter | Criterion | Specific Threshold |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| **C** | Current Quarterly Earnings | EPS up ≥25% vs same quarter prior year |
| **A** | Annual Earnings Growth | EPS up ≥25% for 3 consecutive years |
| **N** | New catalyst | New product / management / all-time high / industry change |
| **S** | Supply & Demand | Smaller float preferred (<25M shares) |
| **L** | Leader not Laggard | Buy the #1 or #2 stock in a leading sector |
| **I** | Institutional Sponsorship | Owned by mutual funds / institutions (recent quarters) |
| **M** | Market Direction | Only buy in a confirmed market uptrend |

**O'Neil's Buy Point Rules:**
- Buy as stock emerges from a *base* (price consolidation area)
- Best base pattern: **Cup-with-Handle** (forms over 7+ weeks on weekly charts)
- Enter on candle close above handle with volume ≥120% of 20-bar average
- Alternative: retest entry — wait for pullback to handle support, enter on resumption
- ⚡ **Hard stop-loss: cut ALL losses at no more than 7–8% below buy point. No exceptions.**

**Cup-with-Handle Dimensions:**
- Cup depth: ideally retraces ≤1/3 of prior advance (can extend to 1/2 in volatile markets)
- Handle: retraces up to 1/3 of cup's advance, but ideally less
- Handle shakeout on low volume is healthy — it scares out weak holders
- Price target: project the depth of the cup upward from the breakout point

### Source: SMB Capital / *One Good Trade* — Mike Bellafiore

**Intraday Entry Framework:**

**Step 1: Identify "Stocks in Play"**
- Pre-market gappers with news catalyst
- Stocks with abnormal volume relative to average
- Stocks hitting 52-week highs or breaking multi-month ranges

**Step 2: Read the Tape**
- Find price levels where more buyers OR more sellers are trapped
- Look for supply/demand imbalance — not just chart patterns
- Order flow tells you *who is in control* at a price level

**Step 3: Wait for Your Setup**
- Bull Flag: tall pole candles → 2-3 red pullback candles → break above flag high
- Momentum VWAP reclaim: price loses VWAP → reclaims it → long on reclaim candle close
- Opening range breakout (first 15–30 min forms range; trade breakout with volume)

**Core Philosophy:**
> "One good trade. Then another good trade. That's all trading is."
> Every trade should have: clear entry reason, defined stop, defined target.

### Source: Momentum & Breakout Research

**Breakout Entry Rules:**
- ⚡ Volume confirmation: ≥50% above 20-day average (minimum); ≥150% above = high-confidence breakout
- Entry candle closes *above* resistance, not just touches it
- **Do NOT chase the initial breakout candle** — wait for retest or next day's open
- False breakouts (fakeouts) are more common on low volume — always check volume first

**Pullback Entry Rules (Preferred for this system):**

- RSI pulls back to 40–50 range (not oversold, just cooling)
- Bullish engulfing or hammer at 20-EMA support
- Volume ticks up on the resumption candle
- If stock pulls through 9-EMA to 20-EMA but holds 20-EMA → still valid entry

## 3. EXIT RULES & PROFIT TAKING

### Source: Synthesized from Livermore, O'Neil, Van Tharp, Mark Douglas

**Complete Exit Decision Framework:**

| Condition | Action | Source |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Price hits -5% from entry | ⚡ Hard stop — exit immediately | ARJUN system rule |
| Price hits -7 to -8% from entry | ⚡ Hard stop — O'Neil rule | O'Neil |
| Price hits +10% target | Take full profit or trail stop | ARJUN system rule |
| Price hits +6% | Trail stop to entry+2% (breakeven+) | ARJUN system rule |
| Time stop: 3:15 PM IST | ⚡ Exit ALL positions unconditionally | ARJUN system rule |
| Trade not moving after 2–3 days | Exit — capital is dead money | Livermore |
| Volume dries up on breakout continuation | Warning signal — tighten stop | Murphy |
| Market trend reverses (broad) | Exit regardless of individual stock | O'Neil |

**Trailing Stop Logic:**
- Activate trailing stop only *after* +6% gain (don't trail too early — shakes you out)
- Trail stop to entry+2% when +6% is reached → you can't lose money now
- If stock continues to +10%, take full exit (original target)
- For larger moves (+15%+), consider partial exit at +10% and trail remainder

**Take Profit Psychology:**
- 🧠 Most traders exit winners too early and hold losers too long (disposition effect)
- Fight this: *predefined* exit targets, not emotional decisions at the moment
- Losses are finite (stop-loss). Gains are uncapped if you trail properly.

## 4. POSITION SIZING & RISK MANAGEMENT

### Source: *Trade Your Way to Financial Freedom* — Van Tharp

**R-Multiples Framework:**
```
R = your risk per trade (entry price − stop-loss price × number of shares)

If stopped at stop-loss:     outcome = -1R
If exited at 2× risk target: outcome = +2R
If exited at 3× risk target: outcome = +3R
```

**Why R-Multiples matter:**
- Forces you to think about risk *before* reward
- System with 40% win rate can be profitable if average winner = 3R and average loser = -1R
- Track all trades in R-multiples — your edge is visible in your R-multiple distribution

**Expectancy Formula:**
```
Expectancy = (Win Rate × Avg Win R) − (Loss Rate × Avg Loss R)
Positive expectancy = profitable system (even with <50% win rate)
```

**System Quality Number (SQN):**
```
SQN = Mean(R-multiples) / StdDev(R-multiples) × √(number of trades)
SQN > 2.0 = good system
SQN > 3.0 = excellent system
```

---

### Source: Kelly Criterion (Mathematical Optimal Position Sizing)

**Kelly Criterion Formula:**
```
Kelly % = W − (1 − W) / R

W = win rate (as decimal, e.g., 0.55 for 55%)
R = win/loss ratio (average win ÷ average loss)
```

**Example:**
- Win rate = 55%, average win = ₹150, average loss = ₹100 → R = 1.5
- Kelly % = 0.55 − (0.45 / 1.5) = 0.55 − 0.30 = 25%
- Bet 25% of capital per trade

**Professional Adjustment — Fractional Kelly:**
- Full Kelly → maximizes long-term growth but produces massive drawdowns
- Most professionals use **Half Kelly (12.5% in above example)**
- Conservative traders use **Quarter Kelly**
- ARJUN uses: max 20% risk per trade (corpus × 0.20) → within half-Kelly territory for most setups

---

### Source: Professional Risk Management Standards

**Portfolio Heat Rules:**
```
Portfolio Heat = sum of (risk per position) across all open trades

Rule: Keep portfolio heat ≤ 5% of total corpus at all times
```

**Daily Loss Limit:**
- ⚡ If day P&L hits -10% of corpus → stop trading for the day
- Professional prop firm standard: 5% daily limit, 20% overall drawdown limit
- ARJUN limit: max 20% per trade, max 2 positions → theoretical max single-day loss = ~40%
  - Mitigated by: stop-losses are mandatory and placed at 5% per trade

**Drawdown Math (Critical to understand):**

| Drawdown | Recovery Required |
|----------|------------------|
| 10% | 11% |
| 20% | 25% |
| 30% | 43% |
| 40% | 67% |
| 50% | 100% |
| 75% | 300% |

> A 50% drawdown needs a 100% gain just to break even.
> This is why capital preservation is always Rule #1.

**Risk Per Trade:**
- Professional standard: 0.5–1% of account equity per trade
- ARJUN standard: 20% of corpus per trade (higher due to aggressive growth target)
- ⚡ This is the acknowledged risk of a 10x-in-7-days target — accepted by operator

## 5. TECHNICAL ANALYSIS PRINCIPLES

### Source: *Technical Analysis of the Financial Markets* — John Murphy

**Murphy's 10 Laws of Technical Trading:**

1. **Map the Trend** — Identify trend on multiple timeframes before trading
2. **Spot the Trend and Go With It** — Trade in trend direction
3. **Find the Low and High of It** — Identify support/resistance
4. **Know How Far to Back Up** — Know Fibonacci retracement levels (38%, 50%, 62%)
5. **Draw the Line** — Trendlines are the simplest, most powerful tool
6. **Follow the Averages** — Price above 200-day SMA = bull; below = bear
7. **Learn the Turns** — Oscillators identify turning points (RSI, Stochastic)
8. **Know the Warning Signs** — MACD divergence, volume divergence signal reversals
9. **Trend or Not a Trend** — ADX >25 = trend; ADX <20 = range-bound (don't use trend strategies)
10. **Know the Confirming Signs** — Volume must confirm price action

**Critical Volume Law:**
> "Volume precedes price. Ensure heavier volume in direction of prevailing trend.
>  Declining volume is a warning that the trend is near completion."

---

### Moving Averages — Key Levels

| MA | Significance |
|----|--------------|
| 9 EMA | Short-term momentum; fast-moving; intraday reference |
| 20 EMA | Key pullback support in uptrends; swing trade reference |
| 50 SMA | Medium-term trend direction; institutional watch level |
| 200 SMA | Long-term trend; bull/bear dividing line |
| VWAP | Intraday fair value; institutional benchmark |

**Golden/Death Cross:**
- 50-day crosses above 200-day → Golden Cross → bullish signal
- 50-day crosses below 200-day → Death Cross → bearish signal

**Moving Averages as Dynamic Support:**
- In uptrend: price pulls back to 20-EMA → buy the bounce
- Price closing below 50-SMA = first warning; below 200-SMA = trend change signal

### MACD Rules

- **Bullish signal**: MACD line crosses above Signal line (buy)
- **Bearish signal**: MACD line crosses below Signal line (sell)
- **Bullish divergence**: Price makes lower low, MACD makes higher low → reversal signal
- **Bearish divergence**: Price makes higher high, MACD makes lower high → weakness signal
- MACD above zero = uptrend momentum; below zero = downtrend momentum

| > 40 | Very strong trend | High probability but may be nearing exhaustion |

## 6. VOLUME ANALYSIS

### Source: John Murphy, O'Neil, Volume Research

**The Volume Hierarchy:**
1. Rising price + Rising volume = Strong healthy uptrend ✅
2. Rising price + Falling volume = Weakening uptrend ⚠️ (suspect)
3. Falling price + Rising volume = Strong distribution, danger ❌
4. Falling price + Falling volume = Consolidation, may reverse soon 🔄

**Breakout Volume Requirements:**
- ⚡ Minimum: volume ≥50% above 20-day average on breakout candle
- High confidence: volume ≥150% above 20-day average
- ⚡ Low-volume breakouts = trap — skip or wait for volume confirmation

**Volume Spike Interpretation:**
- Climactic volume spike on a big candle = potential exhaustion (not continuation)
- Sustained high volume over 2–3 days = institutional accumulation
- Volume drying up after a move = consolidation (base building) — healthy

**Delivery Percentage (India-Specific):**
- ⚡ Delivery % >30% = real investors buying, not speculators
- Delivery % <20% on a big move = likely intraday speculation → less conviction
- High delivery % + high volume = institutional buying signal

**Pattern-Specific Volume Rules:**
| Pattern | Volume Behavior |
|---------|----------------|
| Cup-with-Handle | Contracting volume in handle, surge on breakout |
| Bull Flag | High volume on pole, low volume during flag, high on breakout |
| Triangle | Contracting volume as pattern forms, surge on breakout |
| Gap Up | High volume gap = continuation; low volume gap = likely fills |

## 7. CANDLESTICK PATTERNS

### Source: *Japanese Candlestick Charting Techniques* — Steve Nison

**Reliability Hierarchy (most to least reliable):**

| Pattern | Type | Reliability | Confirmation Required |
|---------|------|-------------|----------------------|
| Bullish/Bearish Engulfing | Reversal | High | Volume + support level |
| Morning Star | Reversal | ~70–75% | Volume on 3rd candle |
| Evening Star | Reversal | ~70–75% | Volume on 3rd candle |
| Hammer (at support) | Reversal | High | Volume + next green candle |
| Shooting Star (at resistance) | Reversal | High | Next red candle confirmation |
| Doji | Indecision | Medium | Direction of next candle |
| Three White Soldiers | Continuation | Medium | Volume must be rising each day |
| Dark Cloud Cover | Reversal | Medium | Volume + next candle |

**Nison's Critical Rule:**
> **Never rely on candlestick patterns in isolation.**
> Combine with: support/resistance levels, trend direction, volume confirmation, and momentum indicators.
> A hammer at support with RSI 35 + volume spike = high probability.
> A hammer in the middle of nowhere = noise.

**Practical Application for ARJUN:**
- ⚡ Bullish engulfing at 20-EMA + volume 1.5x average = valid entry signal
- ⚡ Hammer at key support + RSI 40–50 = pullback entry signal
- 🧠 Always check the *context* — same candle pattern at different locations has different probability

## 8. TREND FOLLOWING

### Source: *Market Wizards* — Jack Schwager (Turtle Traders, Ed Seykota, Paul Tudor Jones)

**Core Formula for Trading Success:**
```
Solid Methodology + Proper Mental Attitude = Trading Success
(Van Tharp / Jack Schwager synthesis)
```

**Turtle Trader Rules (Richard Dennis / William Eckhardt):**
- Trade breakouts to new N-day highs (20-day or 55-day breakouts)
- Use ATR (Average True Range) to set stop-losses
- Pyramid into winning positions — add as trend extends
- Cut losses at 2× ATR; let winners run
- **Result**: Impressive long-term returns despite frequent short-term losses (win rate ~35–40%)

**Ed Seykota's Principles:**
1. Cut losses
2. Ride winners
3. Keep bets small (relative to capital)
4. Follow the rules without question
5. Know when to break the rules (almost never)

**Paul Tudor Jones:**
> "The most important rule of trading is to play great defense, not great offense.
>  I am always thinking about losing money as opposed to making money."

**Key Insight — Trend Following vs. Prediction:**
- Top traders explicitly reject predicting the future
- Instead: React intelligently to what the market is *actually doing*
- Trend following principle: "The trend is your friend until the end."
- 🧠 Don't try to pick tops and bottoms — let price action tell you when the trend is over

**Market Breadth as Trend Confirmation:**
- Healthy uptrend: advancing stocks outnumber declining stocks
- Warning signal: major indices rally but advance-decline line is falling
  → fewer stocks participating → index near a top
- Track: advancing vs. declining stocks, new highs vs. new lows, % stocks above 200-SMA

### Settlement & Delivery Rules

**T+1 Settlement (India, effective 2023):**
- Trades settle next business day (T+1)
- Funds and shares credited T+1 post-market
- Implications: selling on T+1 without receiving delivery requires margin coverage

**F&O Physical Settlement (Post-April 2018):**
- Index derivatives (Nifty, Bank Nifty): Cash settled
- Stock derivatives: Physical settlement at expiry
- 100% contract value required to take/give delivery on expiry
- **Warning**: ITM options at expiry result in physical delivery obligations
